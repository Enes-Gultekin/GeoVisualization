Flood


The picturesque city of Trier, nestled along the banks of the Moselle River, has a rich history dating back to Roman times. However, this historic charm is occasionally overshadowed by the threat of flooding. The Moselle River, known for its scenic beauty and vital role in the region’s economy, can transform into a formidable force during periods of heavy rainfall and severe weather. Recent events have highlighted the vulnerability of Trier and its surrounding areas to flooding, prompting a renewed focus on preparedness and resilience. This webpage aims to provide an overview of the recent flood events, their impact on the community, and the measures being taken to mitigate future risks

In this project I tried to create a simulation which depicts how the city affected by the potential of the flood risk of Moselle river. Following sections shows results and at the end of the page you are going to find these phases of the project.
