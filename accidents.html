Traffic Accidents in NRW State





Results

In 2020, 2,724 people lost their lives in road traffic accidents in Germany. According to preliminary results from the Federal Statistical Office, this was 322 fatalities or 10.6% fewer than in 2019. This means that the number of road deaths reached its lowest level since statistics began more than 60 years ago. The number of injuries also fell in 2020 compared to the previous year, by 14.7% to around 328,000 people.

More detailed results are already available for the period January to November 2020. These show that there were fewer road fatalities in almost all types of road traffic compared to the same period last year. The largest percentage decrease in the first eleven months of 2020 was recorded for fatalities in passenger cars at -14.3% (-176 fatalities). The second largest decrease was recorded in the number of motorcycle fatalities at -8.6% (-51 fatalities), followed by pedestrians at -9.1% (-33 fatalities). In addition, 271 people died on bicycles (without an auxiliary motor), which was 40 fatalities or 12.9% fewer than in the same period in 2019. In contrast, the number of pedelec riders killed between January and November 2020 increased by 22 (19.1%) to 137 people.
